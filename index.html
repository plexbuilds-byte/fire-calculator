FIRE calculator

Estimate whether your invested assets can support your spending at a target age. Nominal return and inflation are combined into a real return for all projections (today’s dollars).

You today

iSets years to target (retirement age − this). Each year until retirement, your portfolio and savings compound at the derived real return.
iStarting balance (cash, brokerage, retirement). Grows as balance × (1 + real return) before contributions—or shrinks if spending exceeds income.
iMoney available each year while working (today’s dollars). With today’s expenses, defines implied savings invested until retirement.
iWhat you spend now, in today’s dollars. Subtracted from income to get annual savings added each year during the accumulation phase.

Retirement target

iEnd of saving and start of drawdown. Projected portfolio and gap vs target are evaluated at this age.
iLast age you plan to spend from the portfolio. With retirement age, sets drawdown years and the spend-down nest egg (level real withdrawals ending near $0).
iYearly spending after you stop working, held constant in purchasing power. Drives portfolio needed at retirement and the perpetual-income comparison.
iNominal portfolio return before inflation (e.g. 8%). Combined with inflation to get the real return used in all math.
iExpected annual rise in cost of living. Real return = (1 + nominal) ÷ (1 + inflation) − 1 so amounts stay in today’s dollars.
iUsed only for the perpetual-income comparison (nest egg = expenses ÷ rate). The main path uses spend-down to $0, not this rate.
